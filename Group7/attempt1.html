Overview

We chose the topic of online gaming because it is a big part of students' daily lives and interests. Many of us play games like Mobile Legends, Minecraft, and Roblox, and we noticed that gaming is often seen only as entertainment. However, we believe it also teaches useful skills such as teamwork, critical thinking, and creativity. By studying this topic, we want to show the positive side of gaming and how it can help students in school and real life.

Process & Journey

  1. Brainstorm & Topic Selection: Compared interests, chose online gaming as our focus.
  2. Thesis & Questions: Final thesis says gaming is more than entertainment; it strengthens teamwork, critical thinking, and creativity. Wrote 5 questions (teamwork, thinking, creativity, communication, problem-solving).
  3. Methods: Built a Google Form (34 responses) and used a short interview guide for live interviews.
  4. Part 4: Considered three content types (YouTube EDU, live strategy streams, podcasts) and did an audience analysis.
  5. Part 5: Selected the most appropriate type (YouTube EDU). Identified a viewer need for skill-focused, balanced gaming content.
  6. Part 6: Drafted Chapter 1 (Background, Statement of the Problem in paragraph form, Significance, References).

Reflection

Students want content that is fun but also teaches real skills from gaming. Time-management is a common concern, so promoting balance is essential.

Audience Analysis

Demographics

The survey was answered by 34 Grade 10 students (ages 14–16), male and female, most of whom are active gamers (Mobile Legends, Minecraft, Roblox).

Beliefs, Opinions & Attitudes

  • Stress relief after school
  • Teamwork & communication in multiplayer games
  • Problem-solving & critical thinking through strategies/decisions
  • Creativity in building/designing (Roblox/Minecraft)
  • Concern: addiction & time management

Student Voices

“Playing Mobile Legends taught me teamwork. It helped me coordinate in group projects.”
“As a team captain in Dota/Valorant, I learned to analyze strategies and make quick decisions under pressure.”
“Minecraft made me think fast and plan step by step—problem-solving in a fun way.”
“In Roblox Studio I built a mini school and custom characters—great for creativity.”
“Gaming is fun, but too much can affect schoolwork. Balance is important.”

Part 5: Finding the Need in Streaming

Three Content Types Considered

  • Educational YouTube videos about gaming
  • Live strategy streams (Twitch/Facebook)
  • Podcasts/discussion episodes about gaming & learning

Most Appropriate Type (Aligned with Audience)

Educational YouTube videos — students already use YouTube; it supports short, skill-focused content that’s easy to access.

Identified Viewer Need

Students need educational gaming content that shows how games build teamwork, critical thinking, and creativity, with reminders about balance & time management.

Platform to Use

YouTube — popular, free, accessible; perfect for combining entertainment with learning.
