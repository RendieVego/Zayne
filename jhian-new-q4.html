1. Summary of Stream

The stream was a live podcast-style talk show focusing on student experiences and how personal interests affect academic performance and social skills.

The host made the stream relatable by sharing personal stories, creating a comfortable and engaging environment.

Interactive features like Canva quizzes, live chat, and Q&A encouraged participation and made the stream more enjoyable.

Overall, the stream was informative, engaging, and supportive for students.

2. Content Analysis

The stream created a welcoming atmosphere with relatable topics such as academic pressure and mental health.

It received an average rating of 4.45, with 63.6% giving a perfect score.

Interactive elements like polls and Q&A helped maintain engagement, while presenter delivery was highly effective.

Overall, the stream successfully combined relevance, interaction, and strong delivery.

3. Areas of Opportunity

Technical issues affected professionalism, especially when a host could not turn on their camera.

Time management can be improved by shortening long discussions.

Promotion should be strengthened through early announcements and teaser content.

Adding expert insights can make the content deeper and more informative.

4. Audience Engagement

The stream generated 183 chat messages, showing active participation.

Engagement averaged 4.27, with more than half rating it 5.

Viewers connected strongly with the topics, creating a sense of community.

However, some viewers were less engaged, suggesting room for improvement.

5. Viewership Analysis

The stream reached 47 views with an average watch time of 6 minutes and 26 seconds.

Peak concurrent viewers reached 9, showing steady interest.

The stream gained 6 new subscribers and received 9 likes.

Despite moderate reach, engagement quality was high, making the stream impactful.
