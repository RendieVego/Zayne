1) Summary of Stream
What the stream was about + how it ran

The stream was a live podcast-style talk show focused on everyday student experiences, especially school life, academic pressure, and mental health. Its goal was to create a safe, relatable space where students could share feelings and realize they are not alone.

The host shared personal experiences about stress and responsibilities, while the technical assistant added interactive features like a live poll. Viewers were encouraged to use chat reactions, and a quick Q&A portion allowed real-time participation.

The tone stayed casual, friendly, and encouraging. After a short break, the stream continued with a more interactive Q&A and ended with a wrap-up message promoting mental health awareness and student support.

2) Content Analysis
What worked well (tone, flow, features)

The stream created a welcoming atmosphere through energetic hosting and relatable topics. The friendly tone made viewers comfortable, and the discussion connected well with student audiences.

The live poll, reactions, and Q&A added structure and helped maintain a smooth flow from introduction to wrap-up, keeping viewers engaged until the end.

3) Areas of Opportunity
What to improve next stream
  • Technical quality: upgrade mic/lighting for a more professional look.
  • Time management: shorten long parts to keep attention steady.
  • Promotion: post announcements early and invite more students to join.
  • Content depth: include teacher/counselor insights for balance.
4) Audience Engagement
How viewers interacted

Viewers interacted actively by sending messages, reacting, and supporting the hosts during the stream. Many students shared that they could relate to the topics, which shows strong connection to the target audience.

This engagement helped build a sense of community and increased loyalty, making future streams more likely to gain support.

5) Viewership Analysis
What the numbers mean

The stream had moderate reach for a student project. The average view duration was 7:14, showing that viewers stayed long enough to watch meaningful parts of the discussion.

It reached a peak of 9 concurrent viewers and gained 5 new subscribers, which indicates growth and interest in future content. The high chat activity shows the audience was engaged, not just watching.

Moderate viewership may be due to limited promotion and a school-focused topic, but the quality of engagement suggests strong impact within the target audience.
